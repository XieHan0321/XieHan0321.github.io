Interaction Analysis: Bathmate (Rich Brown)

User Experience & Visual Interaction Study

1. First Impression

The first noticeable element is the minimal and highly visual interface. The clean layout, strong lighting, and lack of excessive text immediately direct attention toward the product imagery.

2. Two-Minute Interaction Steps

  1. Opened the webpage
  2. Scrolled down
  3. Observed product visuals
  4. Continued scrolling
  5. Paused at detailed render sections
  6. Revisited earlier visuals

3. Time Focus

Most time was spent observing high-quality rendered images due to their detail and visual depth.

4. Most Frequent Action

Scrolling was the most frequent interaction, forming the primary navigation method.

5. Main Goal

The experience aims to transform a sensitive product into a refined, technological, and aesthetically pleasing object.

6. How Goal is Communicated

  • Minimal design reduces discomfort
  • Clinical visual language builds trust
  • Abstract representation avoids explicitness

7. Interaction Over Time

The experience is designed for short engagement (1–3 minutes), primarily as a one-time viewing.

8. Temporal Guidance

Linear scrolling and lack of complex controls encourage quick, continuous viewing rather than deep interaction.

9. Referenced Media Forms

  • Product photography
  • 3D rendering
  • Commercial advertising
  • Industrial design visuals

10. Action Guidance

The design suggests passive viewing rather than active exploration.

11. Emotional Guidance

The experience evokes trust, curiosity, and calmness through controlled visual design.

12. Frustration

The lack of explicit information can lead to confusion about the product’s function.

13. Satisfaction

The polished visual quality and consistent aesthetic provide a satisfying and professional experience.
